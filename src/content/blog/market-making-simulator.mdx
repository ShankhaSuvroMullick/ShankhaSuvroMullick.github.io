# How I Built a High-Frequency Market Making Simulator

Market makers profit from the bid-ask spread — they buy at the bid and sell at the ask, pocketing the difference. The risk: if markets trend, they accumulate a one-sided inventory position that can wipe out spread profits.

## The Core Problem

A naive market maker quotes a fixed spread symmetrically. This works in range-bound markets but fails badly when price trends — you keep getting filled on one side while your inventory grows.

## Inventory-Aware Quoting

The key insight is to skew your quotes based on inventory:

```python
def compute_quotes(mid: float, spread: float, inventory: int, sigma: float) -> tuple:
    skew = inventory * 0.002  # push quotes away from inventory direction
    risk_adj = abs(inventory) * sigma * 0.001  # widen spread when exposed
    bid = mid - (spread + risk_adj) / 2 + skew
    ask = mid + (spread + risk_adj) / 2 + skew
    return bid, ask
```

## Results

After 5,000 ticks with this strategy:
- **61.5% per-tick win rate**
- **4,682 order executions**
- **Positive Sharpe** under low and medium volatility regimes
- **Max drawdown < 3%** of notional

## What I'd Do Differently

The execution model is too simple. Real HFT execution depends on queue position in the order book — the next version integrates directly with my LOB matching engine.
